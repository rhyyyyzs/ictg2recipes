Classic Pork or Chicken Adobo

Pork Adobo is the unofficial national dish of the Philippines. It’s a savory, tangy, and slightly sweet stew where pork is braised in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, peppercorns, and bay leaves. The word adobo comes from the Spanish word adobar (meaning "to marinate"), but the cooking method is indigenous to the Philippines. Long before refrigeration, Filipinos used vinegar and salt to preserve meat in the tropical heat.

Classic Pork or Chicken Adobo
  • Prep time: 10 minutes
  • Cook time: 30–40 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 kg pork belly or chicken thighs (cut into serving pieces)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 whole head garlic (crushed)
  • 2–3 bay leaves
  • 1 tbsp whole peppercorns
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)
  • Oil for searing (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate (optional): Mix the meat, soy sauce, and garlic in a bowl. Let it sit for 30 minutes.
  2. Sear: Heat oil in a pot. Brown the meat on all sides, then remove excess oil.
  3. Simmer: Add marinade, water, peppercorns, and bay leaves. Cover and cook until tender (20 mins for chicken, 40 mins for pork).
  4. Add vinegar: Pour vinegar and do not stir for at least 2 minutes.
  5. Reduce: Simmer uncovered until the sauce thickens to your desired consistency.
