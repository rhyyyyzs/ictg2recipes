The Delicious Taste of Kare-Kare

Kare Kare is a type of Filipino stew with a rich and thick peanut sauce. It is a popular dish in the Philippines served during special occasions. The traditional recipe is composed of ox tail. There are instances wherein both ox tripe and tail are used. The vegetable components of the dish are string beans, eggplant, bok choy, and banana blossoms. Lightly browned toasted ground rice is used to thicken the sauce.

Kare-Kare
  • Prep time: 30 minute
  • Cook time: 2-3 hours

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds oxtail, cut into pieces
  • 6 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ cup peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons ground toasted rice (or rice flour)
  • 1 tablespoon annatto powder (achuete powder)
  • 2 eggplants, sliced
  • 1 bunch string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 banana blossom (puso ng saging), sliced
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • bagoong (fermented shrimp paste), for serving

Instructions:

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. In a large pot, boil the oxtail in water until tender (about 1½ to 2 hours). Skim off any impurities that float on top.
  3. In another pan, heat cooking oil over medium heat. Add garlic and onion; sauté until fragrant.
  4. Add the cooked oxtail and some of its broth. Stir in the peanut butter and annatto powder. Mix well until the sauce becomes smooth.
  5. Add ground toasted rice to thicken the sauce. Let it simmer for about 5–10 minutes while stirring occasionally.
  6. Add the vegetables such as eggplant, string beans, and banana blossom. Cook until vegetables are tender.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot with bagoong on the side.
